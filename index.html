Mobility Trends Across the World During the COVID-19 Pandemic

Data from apple.com

Covid19 has been seriously affecting people’s daily life for 2 years. A lot of people’s transportation choice has changed due to the pandemic. Personally, before I moved to San Francisco in August 2021, I used to commute and travel using public transportation in New York City. However, ever since the pandemic, I haven’t taken NYC’s subway until I moved away. For me, the reason that I stop taking the subway is because I do not want to risk my health to take public transportation. I prefer driving or walking so that there’s less people surrounding me. Apple has published a set of data that features daily changes in requests for directions by transportation type. The data is collected based on the users that uses Apple Maps app. My motivation for selecting this project is that I would like to know if people in the USA feels the same as me — less public transportation during the pandemic. It will also be interesting to know that to what extend covid has influenced people’s transportation decisions.

BEFORE and AFTER most people got 2 doses of Covid vaccination

Conclusion

Covid has significantly affected people's transportation frequency and transportation type. The amount of public transit usage has decreased during the pandemic (especially before most people got the vaccine shots).
Also, the overall transportation frequency has increased after the time period when most people got the vaccine shots. By this, we can assume that people feel safer after getting the vaccination.
